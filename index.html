The THINH MINH Ecosystem:
A Self-Sustaining Urban Economy

Our "Live, Work, and Play" philosophy is not just an urban design concept; it is a strategic business model. By moving beyond isolated industrial parks to fully integrated townships, we create a closed-loop ecosystem. This model generates multiple, overlapping revenue streams, ensures high tenant retention, and creates a highly defensible market position.

Here is how the components of our ecosystem interconnect to drive mutual success:

1. The Foundational Backbone:
Utilities & Infrastructure

Electricity & Clean Water Supply Infrastructure

The Connection: Reliable power and clean water are the lifeblood of any industrial operation. By owning and operating this infrastructure, we guarantee operational continuity for our industrial tenants, which is a massive competitive advantage.

The Investor Value: This provides stable, recurring, and long-term utility revenue while completely de-risking the broader development for both us and our tenants. Furthermore, our alignment with global sustainability standards (UN SDGs) means this infrastructure is designed to be future-proof and resource-efficient.

2. The Economic Engine:
Industry & Logistics

The Industrial Park & Community Logistics

The Connection: The industrial park acts as the economic anchor, attracting multinational corporations and generating mass employment. The integrated logistics network not only serves the heavy supply chain needs of these factories but also supports the daily distribution needs of the community market and retail sectors.

The Investor Value: Attracting top-tier industrial tenants guarantees long-term land leases and utility contracts. The presence of these companies creates the fundamental demographic demand for everything else in the township.

3. The Talent Magnet:
Tiered Residential Solutions

Affordable Housing (Workers) & Commercial Housing (Expats/Managers)

The Connection: A major pain point for industrial tenants globally is labor retention and housing. By providing high-quality, tiered housing directly adjacent to the workspace, we solve this problem for our clients. Factory workers have safe, affordable communities, while foreign experts and local executives have access to premium commercial housing.

The Investor Value: Housing acts as a powerful "stickiness" factor. Industrial tenants are far less likely to relocate if their entire workforce is deeply rooted in the surrounding township. This guarantees high occupancy rates for our residential properties while securing long-term industrial leases.

4. The Community Catalyst:
Active Placemaking & Retail

Shopping Mall & Community Market

The Connection: This is where the "Play" aspect comes to life. The thousands of workers, managers, and their families living in our housing require goods, services, and entertainment. The community market serves daily essential needs, while the shopping mall provides premium retail, dining, and communal spaces for health and vitality.

The Investor Value: This captures the economic output of the township, keeping consumer spending within the ecosystem. It creates a highly lucrative commercial real estate portfolio driven by a captive, built-in audience.

MEIKO Electronics & The Integrated

How Our "Live, Work, and Play" Ecosystem Drives Value for Global High-Tech Manufacturers

Mr. Motojima Katsuhide – CEO MEIKO HOA BINH


MEIKO HOA BINH FACTORY

1. Executive Summary

MEIKO Electronics Co., Ltd., a leading Japanese manufacturer of Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs), has selected our industrial park in Hoa Binh as a critical hub for its global expansion. By plugging into our integrated urban ecosystem, MEIKO dramatically de-risks its supply chain, secures its labor force, and ensures operational continuity for its highly sensitive AI technology manufacturing.

2. The Tenant Profile & Investment Scale

MEIKO is rapidly scaling its operations in Vietnam to meet the surging global demand for AI equipment. Their commitment to the Hoa Binh site is massive and multi-phased. On the afternoon of April 13, Prime Minister Pham Minh Chinh attended the groundbreaking ceremony of a factory project manufacturing electronic printed circuit boards (PCB) of Meiko Electronics Group, Japan, in the industrial park on the left bank of Da River of Da Hop Company.

  • Hoa Binh Plant #1: A state-of-the-art facility focused on Advanced HDI PCBs, covering a site area of approximately 93,000 square meters. This facility represents an investment of approximately 50 billion yen. Recent ground-level updates confirm this phase commands a $200 million USD capital injection and will employ up to 3,000 workers.
  • Hoa Binh Plant #2 (Joint Venture): To further capitalize on AI server demand in Southeast Asia, MEIKO established a Joint Venture with ACCL in November 2025. This new high-layer PCB facility will add another 60,000 square meters of floor area, with production scheduled to begin sequentially from 2027.

3. The Ecosystem in Action: Solving Complex Operational Challenges

For a high-tech manufacturer like MEIKO, an empty plot of land is not enough. Here is how our specific ecosystem components actively support their multi-billion-yen investment:

  • Electricity & Clean Water Supply Infrastructure: The manufacturing of Advanced HDI and High-Layer PCBs for AI Servers requires immense, uninterrupted power and pristine water. In its official financial briefing, MEIKO explicitly cited "leveraging advantages such as electricity supply" as a primary driver for establishing the state-of-the-art factory in Hoa Binh. Our foundational utility infrastructure is the direct catalyst for securing this tenant.

  • Affordable Housing for Workers: MEIKO’s Plant #1 alone requires an immediate workforce of 3,000 people. Our integrated affordable housing solves the most critical bottleneck in modern manufacturing: labor acquisition and retention. By offering safe, walkable residential neighborhoods, MEIKO can guarantee a stable, localized workforce for its mass production lines.

  • Commercial Housing for Expats & Managers: The deployment of advanced manufacturing technologies, such as mSAP HDI, requires heavy oversight from foreign experts and senior Japanese executives. Our commercial housing provides the premium living standards necessary to attract and retain top-tier international talent in Hoa Binh.

  • Shopping Mall, Community Market & Placemaking: A workforce of over 3,000 (plus their families) constitutes a massive micro-economy. The shopping mall and community markets capture this demographic, providing the "Play" and "Live" aspects of our philosophy. This promotes the health, vitality, and well-being of MEIKO's employees, leading to higher productivity and lower turnover.

  • Strategic Partnerships: By aligning with the government's strategic objectives, this integrated township facilitates seamless execution. Once fully operational, MEIKO's facility is projected to contribute up to 4% of Hoa Binh province's total annual budget revenue by 2030, reinforcing the tri-sector synergy between the developer, the tenant, and the state.

4. The Investor Takeaway: The Flywheel Effect

The MEIKO case study perfectly illustrates the financial defensibility of our ecosystem. We are not just leasing 93,000 square meters of dirt; we are providing the essential power, housing the 3,000 workers, and capturing the retail spend of that localized population.

By delivering a holistic "Live, Work, and Play" environment, we ensure that blue-chip tenants like MEIKO remain anchored in our township for decades to come, generating highly predictable, multi-layered revenue streams for our investors.
